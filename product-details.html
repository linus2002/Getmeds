About Cytarabine Injection

Cytarabine belongs to a general group of medicines known as antimetabolites. It is used to treat some types of cancer of the blood (leukemia). Cytarabine works by interfering with the growth of cancer cells, which are eventually destroyed by the body.

Since the growth of normal body cells may also be affected by cytarabine, other unwanted effects will also occur. Some of these may be serious and must be reported to your doctor. Before you begin treatment with cytarabine, you and your doctor should talk about the benefits this medicine will do as well as the risks of using it.

Key Benefits:

  • Effective in acutely inducing remission in leukemia patients.
  • High concentration formulation specifically designed for institutional use.
  • Can be delivered intravenously, subcutaneously, or intrathecally depending on the exact indication.

Directions for Use

This drug is administered by a healthcare professional in a clinical or hospital setting. The dosage and schedule will be determined by your doctor based on your specific condition, body weight, and response to treatment.

Warnings & Precautions

  • Tell your doctor if you have ever had an unusual or allergic reaction to cytarabine or any other medicines.
  • Inform your doctor if you have liver or kidney disease.
  • Do not receive any kind of immunization or vaccination while you are receiving cytarabine without your doctor's approval.
  • Cytarabine can lower the number of white blood cells in your blood, increasing the chance of getting an infection.
  • This medicine may cause birth defects if either the male or female is using it at the time of conception. Use an effective form of birth control.

Possible Side Effects

Check with your doctor immediately if any of the following side effects occur:

  • Bleeding gums
  • Blood in urine or stools
  • Pinpoint red spots on the skin
  • Unusual bleeding or bruising
  • Fever, chills, or sore throat

This is not a complete list of possible side effects. If you notice other effects not listed above, contact your doctor or pharmacist.
